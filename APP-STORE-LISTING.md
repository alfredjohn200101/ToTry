## Description  (4000 char max)
```
To Try is the one place your whole life lives together — your body, your mind, your money, and your spirit — so that keeping track finally becomes something more like counsel.

Most apps fix one thing. To Try holds all of it, and a companion that sees how it connects: why the craving feels strongest on no sleep, why the morning you set shapes the night you face, why the money reclaimed from an old habit is a reason the hard choice was worth it.

It meets you at the moment you feel something — not with a chart, but with presence.

— A COMPANION FOR THE HARD MOMENT —
When you're tempted or struggling, one tap opens a warm, honest presence that stays with you through it: breathing, grounding, catching the thought that gives you permission to slip, and meeting a lapse with grace instead of shame. It's built on approaches clinicians actually use — and it never pretends to replace a real person, a counsellor, or a priest.

— YOUR BODY, ONE LOOP —
Train, fuel, and track as one system, free. Log workouts, scan food (and correct any wrong label once — it's yours forever after), learn your real maintenance calories from your own data, weigh in, and get one honest weekly read: is it working, and the single thing to change.

— A MORNING AND AN EVENING —
Begin the day by choosing who you'll be in it. Close it with an honest look back, grace first. The two are one arc.

— THE FIGHT —
Beat the habits that hold you back. Clean streaks without the shame, the money you reclaim by staying clean, and a gentle check-in at your own hardest hour, learned from your patterns — there before you fall.

— FAITH, FULL BUT NEVER FORCED —
Built honestly from a Christian heart: scripture, prayer, examen, today's readings. A dial turns it up or down, so a person of any belief or none is fully served.

— THE PROMISE —
Free. No ads. No selling your data. It measures whether your life got better, not how long you stared at it — and it's glad to give you back to your life.

Made by one person, Alfred, for anyone quietly trying to be better at anything.
```

To Try is a free, whole-life self-improvement app. Sign in is passwordless: enter an email,
receive a 6-digit code. For review, you can use a real email you control to receive the code.

Native features to test:
